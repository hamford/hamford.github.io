Turn Breakdown (overview)

  1. Setup for Round – Resolve any start‑of‑round effects and ensure command dice are rolled and available.[web:16][web:19][web:45]
  2. Alternate Activations – Players take turns activating one model or fireteam at a time, performing actions such as move, shoot, fight, or special actions.[web:19][web:43]
  3. Actions & Tests – Each activation spends short/long actions, then resolves any required Ranged, Fight, or Survive tests with dice.[web:19][web:42]
  4. End of Round – Once all eligible models have activated, check the mission for scoring, update VP, resolve end‑of‑round effects, then advance to the next round.[web:16][web:42]

Use the Game Tracker tab to track round, VP and key states as you go.

Dice Basics

  • Most tests roll multiple D8; each die that meets or beats a target number counts as a success.[web:16][web:35][web:42]
  • Rolling an 8 is a success and usually grants an extra die (“exploding 8”), which can chain if you roll more 8s.[web:16][web:35][web:37]
  • Ranged and Survive tests are commonly written as “3 Dice Ranged Test (X)” and “3 Dice Survive Test (X)”, meaning you roll 3 dice plus modifiers and compare successes.[web:16][web:35][web:42]

Command Dice Icons (summary)

  • Command dice are rolled at the start of each round; each face shows a symbol you can spend that round to modify actions.[web:40][web:45]
  • Some symbols add extra dice to a Ranged, Fight or Survive test; you must decide how many to spend before rolling.[web:40][web:45]
  • Other symbols grant extra or upgraded actions (for example, additional moves or assaults) depending on the symbol and rules.[web:19][web:40][web:45]

Use this to remember that symbols are spent before rolling and are limited per round; check the official reference for exact icon names and effects.

Dice Modifiers & Positioning

  • Clear Shot / No Obstruction – If the target is clearly visible, attacks usually gain extra dice.[web:16][web:42]
  • Elevated Fire – Shooting from a higher cube often grants +1 die in Ranged tests.[web:16][web:37][web:42]
  • Cover – Targets behind terrain gain defensive benefits; this often adds dice to Survive tests or cancels attack successes.[web:16][web:37][web:42]
  • Close Assault / Crowded Cubes – Being in the same cube as enemies or friends can give bonuses or penalties depending on the situation and weapons.[web:16][web:41][web:42]

Combine these with command dice to quickly estimate how many dice to roll before an attack or defense.

Keyword Quick Reference

Short reminders only; always check the official rules if there is any doubt.[web:16][web:35][web:36]

Common Keywords (examples)

Explosive / Grenade
Often trigger additional tests (for example, Frag (n)) after a hit; nearby models may make Survive tests with limited modifiers or rerolls.[web:16][web:36][web:44]
Frag (n)
After a successful hit, roll (n) dice for attack strength, then models in the target area make Survive tests; damage is based on the difference in successes.[web:16][web:36][web:44]
High Ground
Attacker in an elevated cube typically gains an extra die to Ranged tests against lower targets.[web:16][web:37][web:42]
Cover
Target obscured by terrain/models gains a defensive benefit; may add dice to Survive tests or cancel attack successes.[web:16][web:37][web:42]
Crouch / Defensive Posture
Grants a defensive bonus, often via extra Survive dice or modifiers when the model is shot at.[web:16][web:43]
Melee / Brawler‑style keywords
Provide bonuses in Fight tests, sometimes based on friendly models in the same cube or weapon traits.[web:16][web:35][web:41]
